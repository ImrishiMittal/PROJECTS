GEOGRAPHICAL LOCATION

Dadra and Nagar Haveli is a Union Territory in western India, located between the states of Gujarat and Maharashtra. It lies on the western coast of India and is surrounded by the Valsad district of Gujarat to the west, north, and east, and by the Palghar district of Maharashtra to the south. Here’s a detailed look at its geographical location and features:

1. Location and Boundaries
  • Latitude and Longitude: Dadra and Nagar Haveli is situated approximately between 20.1809° N latitude and 73.0169° E longitude.
  • Bordering States: The Union Territory is bordered by Gujarat on three sides and Maharashtra to the south.
  • Distance from Major Cities: Silvassa, the capital of Dadra and Nagar Haveli, is around 180 kilometers from Mumbai and 120 kilometers from Surat, making it easily accessible from these major cities.
2. Topography
  • Landscape: The terrain of Dadra and Nagar Haveli is mostly undulating with small hills, dense forests, and rivers, giving it a scenic and rustic landscape. It is a hilly area covered with dense forests, especially towards the eastern side, as it is part of the Western Ghats range.
  • Forested Area: Approximately 40% of the Union Territory is covered with forests, making it one of the more ecologically rich areas in western India. The forests are mostly tropical, consisting of various species of trees and a wide range of flora and fauna.
3. Major Rivers
  • Damanganga River: The Damanganga River flows through Dadra and Nagar Haveli and is the major river in the region. It originates in the Western Ghats, flowing through the territory and into the Arabian Sea. The river provides irrigation and is crucial for the local agriculture.
  • Endemic Wildlife and Biodiversity: The river system and forests make the region a rich biodiversity zone, home to several species of animals, birds, and plants.
4. Climate
  • Tropical Climate: The territory experiences a tropical climate, with hot summers, a humid monsoon season, and mild winters. The average temperature ranges from 25°C to 32°C.
  • Monsoon Influence: The southwest monsoon brings heavy rains from June to September, contributing to the lush greenery and agricultural productivity of the region.
5. Flora and Fauna
  • Biodiversity: Dadra and Nagar Haveli has a rich array of flora and fauna, particularly in its forested areas. It is home to various species of birds, animals, and plants that thrive in the humid, forested environment.
  • Protected Areas: The Union Territory has several protected areas and wildlife sanctuaries, which help in conserving its natural heritage.
6. Agriculture and Economy
  • Agricultural Land: The region’s fertile land and favorable climate allow for the cultivation of crops like rice, ragi, jowar, and various fruits.
  • Industrial Influence: Despite its rural and forested landscape, Dadra and Nagar Haveli has a significant industrial presence, especially in and around Silvassa, which has grown as an industrial hub due to favorable tax policies.
7. Connectivity
  • Accessibility: Dadra and Nagar Haveli is well-connected by road, with National Highway 848A passing through it. Although it doesn’t have its own airport or railway station, the proximity to major cities like Mumbai and Surat ensures good accessibility.
  • Nearest Airport and Railway Stations: The nearest airports are in Mumbai and Surat, while Vapi, in Gujarat, is the nearest railway station, located about 18 kilometers from Silvassa.
Conclusion

Dadra and Nagar Haveli’s geographic location between Gujarat and Maharashtra, along with its forested terrain, rivers, and favorable climate, makes it a unique region with rich biodiversity and scenic beauty. It is an ecologically significant area with a blend of tribal culture, natural landscapes, and industrial growth.
