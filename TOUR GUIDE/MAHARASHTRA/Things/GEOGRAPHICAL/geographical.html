Geographical Location of Maharashtra

Maharashtra is located in the western and central part of India and is one of the country's largest states, both in terms of area and population. Covering approximately 307,713 square kilometers, it shares its borders with several states and features diverse geographical characteristics, from plateaus to coastal plains. Here's a closer look at its geographical details:

2. Boundaries
  • North: Gujarat and Madhya Pradesh
  • East: Chhattisgarh
  • South: Karnataka and Goa
  • West: The Arabian Sea
  • Northwest: The Union Territories of Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu
3. Topography
  • Western Ghats (Sahyadri Range): Running parallel to the Arabian Sea coast, this mountain range forms a natural divide between the Konkan coast and the Deccan Plateau. It is a biodiversity hotspot and has several important hill stations like Mahabaleshwar, Lonavala, and Matheran.
  • Deccan Plateau: The eastern part of Maharashtra consists of the Deccan Plateau, characterized by its volcanic basalt soil, which makes it suitable for agriculture. It comprises flat-topped plateaus and rugged terrain.
  • Konkan Coast: The narrow coastal strip between the Arabian Sea and the Western Ghats is known as Konkan. It is known for its scenic beaches, lush greenery, and fertile soil.
4. Major Rivers
  • Godavari: Known as the "Ganga of the South," it is Maharashtra's longest river, flowing eastward across the state.
  • Krishna, Bhima, Tapi, and Narmada: Other significant rivers flow through the region, contributing to its irrigation and agriculture.
5. Climate
  • Maharashtra experiences a tropical climate with three main seasons: summer (March to June), monsoon (June to September), and winter (October to February). The coastal region of Konkan has a humid climate, while the interior regions experience a more extreme climate with hotter summers and colder winters.
6. Key Cities
  • Mumbai: The state's capital, India's financial hub and one of the world's most densely populated cities.
  • Pune, Nagpur, Nashik, Aurangabad, and Kolhapur are other major cities.

In conclusion, Maharashtra's geographical location and features make it a land of contrasts, with bustling urban centers, scenic coasts, lush hills, and agriculturally rich plains, contributing to its economic, cultural, and natural significance.
