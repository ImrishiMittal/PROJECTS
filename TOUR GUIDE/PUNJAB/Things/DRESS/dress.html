DRESS

Traditional dress in Indian Punjab is vibrant, practical, and reflective of the region’s cultural diversity and agricultural heritage. The clothing is designed to suit the climate, which is mostly hot and semi-arid, and the lifestyles of the people, including the working classes, farmers, and urban dwellers. Here's a breakdown of the traditional dress worn by men and women in Indian Punjab:

1. Men's Traditional Dress
Kurta-Pajama
  • Kurta: A long tunic, often made of cotton or linen, with a straight cut, reaching down to the knees or below. It can have different designs, from plain to embroidered, depending on the occasion.
  • Pajama: A pair of loose-fitting trousers, usually made of cotton or silk, worn with the kurta. The pajama is tied at the waist with a drawstring.
Salwar-Kameez
  • Kameez: A long tunic, similar to a kurta but often slightly more tailored.
  • Salwar: A baggy pant, gathered at the ankles, similar to a harem pant.
  • This outfit is often worn by men in rural areas and is also common in urban settings for casual wear.
Sherwani
  • The sherwani is a long coat-like garment that is often richly embroidered. It is typically paired with a churidaar (a tight-fitting trouser) or salwar. The sherwani is a symbol of luxury and elegance and is usually worn with a turban for cultural and religious significance.
Pagri (Turban)
  • The pagri, or turban, is a significant and proud symbol of Punjabi culture, especially among the Sikh community. The turban is worn by Sikh men as a sign of respect, spirituality, and identity. The turban is often wrapped around the head in a traditional manner and comes in various colors, with white being common for religious occasions and bright colors for festive occasions.
Jodhpuri Suit
  • A Jodhpuri suit is another formal outfit worn by men in Punjab, especially at weddings. It is a tailored jacket with intricate embroidery and can be worn with either trousers or a salwar.
Footwear
  • Juttis or Mojaris: Traditional footwear, often made of leather or fabric, with intricate embroidery. These are commonly worn with the kurta-pajama and salwar-kameez.
  • Sandals or chappals are often worn in casual settings.
2. Women's Traditional Dress
Salwar-Kameez
  • Like men, women also wear salwar-kameez as a traditional outfit. The kameez is often more stylish, with embroidery, mirror work, or embellishments. It is paired with a salwar or churidar (a tighter-fitting version of the salwar), and a dupatta (scarf).
  • Churidar: A fitted trouser that gathers around the ankles, commonly worn in urban settings.
  • The dupatta is a long scarf worn over the shoulders and head, which adds to the elegance and is also used for modesty.
Lehenga-Choli
  • The lehenga-choli is a traditional attire worn during weddings, festivals, and other celebrations. It consists of:
    • Lehenga: A long skirt, often heavily embroidered or decorated, and worn with a choli (blouse).
    • Choli: A short, fitted blouse, typically with intricate embroidery or designs, often made of silk or satin.
    • Dupatta: A long scarf draped over the head or around the neck, completing the look. The lehenga-choli is often worn at weddings or during cultural festivities.
Anarkali Dress
  • The Anarkali dress is a long, flowing gown-style outfit that has gained popularity in Punjabi culture for formal and festive occasions. It is typically worn with a churidar or leggings, with the skirt part of the dress often having pleats that give it a voluminous, regal look.
Sari
  • Although less common in rural areas, the sari is still worn by some women in urban Punjab for special occasions, such as weddings and religious ceremonies. The sari consists of a long piece of cloth (usually 6-9 yards) that is draped around the body and paired with a blouse.
Dupatta
  • A dupatta is an essential part of traditional Punjabi women’s attire. It is a long scarf worn with the salwar-kameez, lehenga-choli, or sari, adding both elegance and modesty. The dupatta may be made of cotton, silk, chiffon, or wool, depending on the season and occasion.
3. Traditional Fabrics and Embroidery
Phulkari
  • One of the most important traditional forms of embroidery in Punjab is Phulkari, which means "flower work." This technique is used to create vibrant, intricate floral patterns on fabrics like shawls, scarves, dupattas, and saris. Phulkari embroidery is especially popular for bridal wear.
Bagh
  • Bagh: A type of embroidery similar to Phulkari, but with a different design approach. It’s generally used for more intricate, symmetrical patterns on garments.
Cotton and Khadi
  • Given the hot climate of Punjab, cotton is a popular fabric choice for daily wear. Khadi, a handwoven fabric, is also commonly worn and valued for its comfort and simplicity.
Silk and Satin
  • For special occasions, women wear silk or satin outfits, such as the lehenga-choli or Anarkali, due to their luxurious feel and appearance.
4. Traditional Clothing for Special Occasions
Weddings
  • Weddings in Punjab are grand affairs and involve rich traditional attire. For brides, the lehenga-choli or Anarkali is often worn, and the bride is adorned in elaborate jewelry, including maang tikka, chooda (red bangles), and nath (nose ring).
  • The groom typically wears a sherwani with a turban and mojari shoes, often accompanied by a sehra (a veil made of flowers) on the head.
Festivals
  • During major festivals like Baisakhi, Diwali, and Lohri, both men and women dress in their finest clothes, often wearing traditional Punjabi attire such as salwar-kameez, lehenga-choli, and sherwani for men. Women wear colorful phulkari shawls and vibrant dupattas.
5. Modern Clothing

In urban areas like Chandigarh, Amritsar, and Ludhiana, Western-style clothing such as jeans, shirts, T-shirts, and dresses are also popular, especially for younger generations. However, traditional attire still plays a significant role in daily life and cultural events.

Conclusion

Punjabi dress reflects the region’s rich cultural heritage and its agricultural roots. The clothing is vibrant, practical, and closely tied to religious customs, festivals, and special occasions. While traditional attire such as salwar-kameez, lehenga-choli, sherwani, and kurta-pajama continues to be worn with pride, modern influences can also be seen in everyday clothing. However, no matter the occasion, traditional clothing remains a symbol of Punjabi identity, warmth, and hospitality.
