DRESS

The traditional dress of Manipur

The traditional dress of Manipur reflects its rich cultural heritage and is marked by distinct styles for both men and women, often worn during festivals, ceremonies, and other important events.

Women's Dress
  1. Phanek: The Phanek is the most iconic and traditional attire worn by Manipuri women. It is a rectangular piece of cloth, typically made of cotton or silk, which is wrapped around the waist like a sarong and tied securely. The Phanek is often decorated with intricate patterns and borders and is worn with a blouse or top.
  2. Innaphi: The Innaphi is a traditional shawl worn by women, draped over the shoulders or around the body. It is typically woven from silk and features beautiful designs. The Innaphi is often worn during special occasions, festivals, or formal gatherings, adding a layer of elegance to the attire.
  3. Rikong: This is a traditional headpiece worn by women during certain festivals and ceremonies. It is a decorated headdress that adds to the cultural charm of the attire.
  4. Accessories: Women in Manipur often adorn themselves with various traditional jewelry pieces, such as necklaces, earrings, bangles, and anklets made from silver or gold. They may also wear traditional hairpieces or headbands during special occasions.
Men's Dress
  1. Lungi (or Dhoti): Men typically wear a lungi or dhoti, which is a piece of cloth tied around the waist in a way similar to the lungi or mundu worn in southern India. This is the traditional lower garment, worn with a simple shirt or tunic on top.
  2. Phirei: The Phirei is another traditional cloth worn by men, particularly in rural areas. It is similar to the lungi and is worn around the waist and secured with a knot.
  3. Puja Melei: For formal and religious occasions, men often wear the Puja Melei, a more ceremonial outfit, which is a traditional dress made from silk.
  4. Accessories: Men may also wear a Kamarbandh (waistband) or Mekhala (traditional belt), along with silver or gold jewelry for special occasions.
Footwear

Khudei: Both men and women wear Khudei, a traditional footwear made from wood or leather, which is simple but often stylish.

Traditional attire in Manipur is a significant part of its cultural identity. These dresses are not just garments, but symbols of tradition, heritage, and pride for the people of Manipur, often worn during festivals like Yaoshang, Ningol Chakouba, and Lai Haraoba.
