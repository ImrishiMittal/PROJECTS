GEOGRAPHICAL LOCATION

**Geographical Location of Karnataka:** Karnataka is located in the southern part of India and is bordered by several states and the Arabian Sea. It is strategically positioned with a varied landscape that includes coastal areas, plateaus, forests, and mountain ranges. Below is a breakdown of Karnataka's geographical location: 1. **Borders**: - **North**: Maharashtra - **South**: Tamil Nadu and Kerala - **East**: Andhra Pradesh - **West**: Arabian Sea 2. **Coastline**: Karnataka has a long coastline of about **320 kilometers** along the western edge, along the **Arabian Sea**. This coastline is known for its beautiful beaches, including **Gokarna**, **Karwar**, and **Udupi**. 3. **Geography**: - The state is characterized by diverse landscapes. The **Western Ghats** (also known as **Sahyadri**) form the western border of the state, contributing to its rich biodiversity and tropical climate. - The **Deccan Plateau** covers most of the state's area and is marked by a series of hills and valleys. - The **Eastern Ghats** are present in the southeastern parts of Karnataka. - The **Karnataka Plateau** and its surrounding areas are rich in minerals and provide the state with a fertile agricultural belt. 4. **Rivers**: Karnataka is home to several important rivers, such as the **Cauvery**, **Krishna**, and **Tungabhadra**, which flow through the state and play an essential role in agriculture and the overall ecosystem. 5. **Climate**: Karnataka experiences a tropical climate with three distinct seasons: summer, monsoon, and winter. - **Summer** (March to May) is hot, with temperatures often rising above 35°C. - **Monsoon** (June to September) brings heavy rains, especially to the coastal regions and the Western Ghats. - **Winter** (November to February) is relatively cooler, with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 25°C. Karnataka's geographical location plays a key role in its cultural diversity, agricultural productivity, and natural resources, making it one of the most important states in India.
