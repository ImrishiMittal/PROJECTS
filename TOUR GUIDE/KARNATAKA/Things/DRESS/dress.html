DRESS

**Dress of Karnataka:** The traditional dress of Karnataka reflects the state's diverse cultural heritage, with distinct variations for men and women, influenced by the geographical regions and the rich history of the state. The attire is often vibrant and intricately designed, aligning with the state's colorful festivals and cultural celebrations. ### **Women’s Traditional Dress:** 1. **Saree:** - The **saree** is the most popular traditional attire for women in Karnataka. Women often wear sarees made of cotton or silk, especially during festivals and ceremonies. The **Mysore silk saree**, known for its elegance and fine texture, is one of the most famous types of saree in the state. - The **Kanjivaram sarees**, though originally from Tamil Nadu, are also widely worn by women in Karnataka. - The **Kannada-style saree draping** involves wearing the saree in a simple yet elegant manner, with pleats tucked in at the front and the pallu draped over the shoulder. 2. **Choli and Langa:** - In some parts of Karnataka, especially in the **coastal region**, women wear a traditional outfit called the **Langa** and **Choli**. The **Langa** is a long skirt, while the **Choli** is a fitted blouse. - This attire is often seen during traditional dance forms like **Yakshagana** and on festive occasions. 3. **Dupatta:** - The dupatta (a long scarf) is often worn with a **salwar kameez** or **chudidar**, adding a touch of elegance to the attire. 4. **Jewelry:** - Women in Karnataka wear traditional jewelry made of gold, silver, and sometimes, precious stones. The **Mysore Jewels** are a significant part of Karnataka's cultural heritage, including ornate necklaces, bangles, ear tops, and headpieces. - The **Jhumkas (earrings)**, **Nose pins**, **Har (necklace)**, and **Vaddanam (waist belt)** are commonly worn in special occasions. 5. **Footwear:** - Women generally wear **ballet flats**, **chappals**, or traditional **Kolhapuri chappals**, which are crafted from leather and are extremely comfortable. ### **Men’s Traditional Dress:** 1. **Dhoti or Panche:** - The traditional attire for men in Karnataka is the **dhoti** or **panche**, particularly worn during weddings, festivals, or religious ceremonies. It is usually paired with a shirt or a **kurta**. - The **purna dhoti** is tied around the waist and is a significant part of the attire for special occasions, especially in rural and semi-rural regions. 2. **Kurta and Pyjama:** - Men often wear a **kurta** (a long tunic) paired with **pyjamas** or **lungis** for daily wear. These are generally worn during formal occasions and festivals like **Dasara**. - The kurta is sometimes paired with a **sherwani** during weddings and other cultural celebrations. 3. **Sherwani and Suits:** - During weddings or important cultural events, men may wear a **sherwani**, which is a long coat-like garment, often paired with **churidars** or **leggings**. - Some urban areas also witness men wearing **western-style suits** for formal events. 4. **Accessories:** - Traditional attire is often complemented with **headgear** like **mangalsutra** (for ceremonial purposes), **angavastram**, or **shawls**. - In rural areas, men may wear a **turban** or **headscarf**. ### **Modern Attire:** - In urban areas like **Bengaluru**, **Hubli**, and **Mysuru**, the influence of modern fashion is evident, with both men and women often opting for western clothing such as **jeans, shirts, trousers, skirts**, and **dresses**. - However, traditional attire is still worn on festivals, special occasions, and cultural functions, keeping the cultural heritage alive in daily life. In summary, the traditional dress of Karnataka is characterized by elegant **sarees** and **silk fabrics** for women, and **dhoti**, **kurta**, and **sherwanis** for men. The attire varies slightly across regions, but all forms are a reflection of Karnataka’s rich culture and tradition.
