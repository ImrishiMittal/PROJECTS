DRESS

Khasi Traditional Dress
  • Women: The Jainsem is a two-piece drape, paired with the Tapmohkhlieh scarf.
  • Festive Attire: During festivals, they wear the Kynthei headdress with coral and gold jewelry.
  • Men: The Jymphong coat, worn with a dhoti, is traditional men's attire.
Jaintia Traditional Dress
  • Women: The attire resembles the Khasi Jainsem but includes a Kyrshah headscarf.
  • Men: They wear a simple dhoti with optional jackets or shawls.
Garo Traditional Dress
  • Women: The Dakmanda skirt is vibrant and intricately designed.
  • Festive Wear: Worn with traditional blouses and adorned with natural bead necklaces.
  • Men: A loincloth or dhoti, often accompanied by sleeveless coats.
Ornaments and Accessories
  • Jewelry: Made of gold, silver, and beads, symbolizing tribal heritage.
Modern Influences

Traditional attire remains significant during festivals, while modern influences are blending with daily wear.
