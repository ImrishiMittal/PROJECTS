DRESS

Traditional Dress of Sikkim

Sikkim's traditional dress is vibrant and reflects its diverse cultures. Here are some key elements of traditional attire from different communities:

Key Elements of Traditional Attire
1. Bhutia Dress

Men: Typically wear a chuba, a long woolen robe, often paired with a daura (a long shirt) and khamar (headgear). They might also wear a bakkhu, a traditional shawl.

Women: Usually wear a bakhu, which is similar to a chuba but more fitted, and often paired with a churidar or sari. The attire is complemented by colorful jewelry and traditional ornaments.

2. Lepcha Dress

Men: Often wear a chuba with a topi (hat) made from wool or other materials.

Women: Traditionally wear a sethru, a long dress with a shawl, and a rakthang, which is a wrap-around skirt. They also adorn themselves with handmade jewelry.

3. Nepali Dress

Men: Commonly wear a daura-suruwal, which consists of a long shirt (daura) and tapered trousers (suruwal). They often wear a topi (cap) for formal occasions.

Women: Typically wear a gunyo-cholo (a blouse with a sari-like wrap) or a gunyu (sari), along with colorful jewelry, especially during festivals and ceremonies.

4. Accessories and Jewelry

Traditional attire is often accessorized with intricate jewelry, including earrings, necklaces, and bangles, made from silver and other materials.

5. Influence of Climate

Due to Sikkim's varied climate, especially in higher altitudes, traditional dresses are often made from warm, durable materials, making them both practical and stylish.

Conclusion

Overall, the traditional dress of Sikkim showcases the rich cultural heritage of its people, blending colors, patterns, and styles that are unique to each community.
