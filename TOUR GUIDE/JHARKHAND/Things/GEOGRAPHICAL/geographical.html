Geographical Location of Jharkhand

Geographical Location of Jharkhand:

Jharkhand is located in the eastern part of India and is characterized by its diverse terrain of hills, forests, and plateaus. The state, often referred to as the "Land of Forests," was carved out of the southern part of Bihar on November 15, 2000. Here are key points regarding its geographical features:

  • Boundaries: Jharkhand shares its borders with five states:
    • North: Bihar
    • West: Uttar Pradesh and Chhattisgarh
    • South: Odisha
    • East: West Bengal
  • Latitude and Longitude: The state is approximately located between 21°58'N to 25°18'N latitude and 83°22'E to 87°57'E longitude.
  • Area: Jharkhand covers an area of around 79,714 square kilometers, making it one of the smaller states in terms of area in India.
  • Topography: The state's terrain is predominantly hilly and forested, with several plateau regions. The Chota Nagpur Plateau forms the backbone of its geography, covering a significant portion of the state. This region is known for its rich mineral resources.
  • Rivers: Several rivers flow through Jharkhand, the most significant being the Damodar, Subarnarekha, Koel, and Son rivers. These rivers play a vital role in irrigation and hydropower generation.
  • Climate: Jharkhand experiences a tropical climate with three main seasons: summer, monsoon, and winter. Summers can be hot, with temperatures ranging from 35°C to 45°C, while winters are relatively mild, with temperatures between 5°C and 25°C.
  • Forests and Biodiversity: The state is home to a rich variety of flora and fauna, with forests covering about 29% of its total area. The forests are primarily deciduous, with sal, mahua, and bamboo being commonly found trees. Jharkhand's wildlife includes elephants, tigers, leopards, deer, and various species of birds.
  • Mineral Wealth: Jharkhand is renowned for its mineral resources, particularly coal, iron ore, copper, bauxite, and uranium, making it one of India's most mineral-rich states. Mining activities significantly influence the state's economy and landscape.

    • Jharkhand's geographical diversity contributes to its cultural richness and economic significance, as well as offering natural beauty with its lush greenery, hills, waterfalls, and wildlife.
