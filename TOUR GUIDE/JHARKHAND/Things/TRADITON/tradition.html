TRADITION

**Tradition of Jharkhand:** Jharkhand, known for its rich tribal heritage and vibrant customs, boasts traditions that are deeply rooted in the state’s diverse communities and historical influences. These traditions reflect the unique lifestyle, beliefs, and cultural practices of Jharkhand’s people, primarily driven by its indigenous tribes, who constitute a significant portion of the population. Here’s a closer look at the traditions of Jharkhand: ### **1. Tribal Customs and Practices:** Jharkhand is home to various tribes, such as the **Santhal, Munda, Oraon, Ho, and Paharia**, each with their distinct traditions and way of life. The tribes maintain a close connection with nature and follow unique practices: - **Nature Worship**: Many indigenous communities worship natural elements like trees, rivers, and animals, often holding sacred ceremonies to honor nature. Festivals like **Sarhul**, a spring festival, involve worshipping the Sal tree to thank nature for its bounties. - **Totemism**: Tribal groups often identify themselves with particular totems such as plants, animals, or symbols that are significant to their lineage. ### **2. Festivals and Celebrations:** Jharkhand’s festivals showcase its vibrant cultural heritage: - **Karma Festival**: Celebrated by the Oraon, Munda, and other tribes, this festival involves worshipping the **Karam tree** for prosperity and good fortune. Traditional folk dances and songs, performed around the Karam tree, are central to the celebrations. - **Jitia Festival**: Primarily observed by women, this festival is dedicated to the welfare and longevity of their children. It involves fasting, prayers, and rituals. - **Maghe Parab**: Celebrated by the Munda tribe to mark the harvest season and thank the deities for their blessings. - **Tusu Festival**: A harvest festival observed by various tribal groups during Makar Sankranti, featuring songs and folk traditions. ### **3. Dance and Music:** - **Folk Dances**: Dance plays a pivotal role in Jharkhand’s traditions, with performances like **Chhau, Paika, Jhumar, and Santhali dance**. Chhau, a masked dance form, has roots in martial traditions and storytelling. - **Traditional Music**: Tribal communities sing traditional songs during celebrations and rituals, accompanied by instruments like **Mandar, Dhol, Nagara, and Bansuri (flute)**. ### **4. Handicrafts and Art:** - **Sohrai and Khovar Art**: Jharkhand is known for its distinctive wall paintings, often drawn during festivals like Sohrai (a cattle and harvest festival) and Khovar (wedding season). The art features intricate geometric and natural designs. - **Wood Carving and Bamboo Crafts**: Tribal artisans create beautiful wooden sculptures, masks, and bamboo crafts, reflecting their artistic heritage. ### **5. Marriage Customs:** Traditional marriages in Jharkhand involve elaborate rituals, including **community feasts, songs, and dances**. Dowry is generally discouraged among many tribal communities, with customs emphasizing communal participation and blessings. ### **6. Clothing Traditions:** Traditional attire varies among different tribes and is often adorned with beads, silver jewelry, and intricate patterns. **Men typically wear dhotis, while women drape saris**, sometimes paired with colorful blouses. The clothing reflects their simple yet vibrant lifestyle. ### **7. Social Traditions and Governance:** - **Akhra System**: An important part of tribal social life, **Akhra** refers to an open space used for gatherings, dances, and discussions. It serves as a cultural and social hub. - **Panchayat System**: Tribal communities often follow a traditional panchayat system for resolving disputes and community decision-making. Elders hold respected positions and play crucial roles in maintaining social order. ### **8. Agricultural and Forest-Based Traditions:** The people of Jharkhand have traditionally depended on **farming, hunting, and forest produce**. Many customs, songs, and dances are centered around agricultural cycles, emphasizing their respect for land and its produce. ### **9. Food Traditions:** Traditional food is simple and often includes forest products, rice, millet, **handia (rice beer)**, and a variety of herbs and greens sourced from forests. Sharing food is a communal practice, strengthening social bonds within communities. Jharkhand’s traditions exemplify a harmonious coexistence with nature, a strong sense of community, and a deep respect for heritage. Its rituals, dances, music, and festivals continue to thrive, fostering a unique and vibrant cultural identity.
