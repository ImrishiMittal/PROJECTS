GEOGRAPHICAL LOCATION

Tripura is a state located in the northeastern part of India, bordered by Bangladesh to the west, south, and east, and the Indian states of Assam and Manipur to the north. Here’s a detailed overview of its geographical location:
1. Geographical Coordinates
  • Latitude: 23.5° N
  • Longitude: 91.5° E
2. Borders and Neighboring States
  • Bangladesh: Tripura shares a significant portion of its border with Bangladesh, making it strategically important for trade and cross-border relations. This is the longest international border Tripura shares with any country, covering over 800 kilometers.
  • Assam: To the north of Tripura, Assam is another northeastern state, which provides a road link to the rest of India.
  • Manipur: Manipur lies to the eastern side of Tripura.
3. Topography
  • Hills and Plains: Tripura is predominantly a hilly state, with a series of small hills and valleys, but also contains flat plains and plateaus. The state is part of the Tripura Plateau, which is situated in the Barak Valley region.
  • Rivers and Water Bodies: Several rivers flow through the state, with the Haora (or Howrah) and Khowai rivers being the most prominent. These rivers form an integral part of the state's irrigation system and provide natural beauty.
4. Climate
  • Tropical Climate: Tripura experiences a tropical climate with three distinct seasons:
    • Summer (March to June): Hot and humid, with temperatures ranging from 30°C to 40°C.
    • Monsoon (June to September): Heavy rainfall due to the southwest monsoon, leading to lush greenery.
    • Winter (October to February): Pleasant and mild, with temperatures ranging between 10°C and 20°C.
  • The climate is conducive to rich biodiversity, with dense forests and a variety of flora and fauna.
5. Elevation
  • The state’s elevation ranges from around 30 meters above sea level in the plains to over 900 meters in the hilly regions, particularly in the Maha Tripura Sundari Temple area and surrounding hills.
6. Notable Geographic Features
  • Agartala: The capital city of Tripura is situated in the western part of the state, close to the Bangladesh border. It is the largest city and serves as the commercial, political, and cultural hub of the state.
  • Ujjayanta Palace: Located in Agartala, this historical palace is one of the most iconic landmarks in Tripura, reflecting the state’s royal heritage.
  • Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary: Located about 20 km from Agartala, this sanctuary is a biodiversity hotspot, home to various species of birds, reptiles, and mammals.
  • Jampui Hills: A scenic range of hills located in the northeastern part of the state, known for their pleasant climate, lush landscapes, and orange orchards.
7. Access and Connectivity
  • Roads: Tripura is well-connected by road to Assam and Manipur. National Highway 8 (NH8) is the primary highway that connects Tripura to the rest of India.
  • Air: The Maharaja Bir Bikram Airport in Agartala connects the state to major cities like Kolkata, Guwahati, and New Delhi. It is the main gateway for air travel.
  • Railways: The railway network connects Tripura to the rest of India, with major stations like Agartala and Udaipur.
Conclusion

Tripura is strategically located in the northeastern corner of India, bordered by Bangladesh, Assam, and Manipur. Its varied topography, ranging from hills to plains, combined with its tropical climate, makes it a unique and ecologically rich state. Its geographical location also influences the state’s culture, history, and trade relations, making it an important hub in the Northeast region of India.
