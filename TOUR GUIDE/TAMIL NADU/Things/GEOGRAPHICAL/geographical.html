GEOGRAPHICAL LOCATION

Tamil Nadu is located in the southernmost part of India. Here are some key geographical details about the state:
1. Boundaries
  • North: Tamil Nadu is bordered by the states of Karnataka to the northwest and Andhra Pradesh to the north.
  • East: The state has a long coastline along the Bay of Bengal to the east.
  • South: Kanyakumari, the southernmost point of mainland India, marks the end of the state, where the Indian Ocean, Arabian Sea, and Bay of Bengal meet.
  • West: Kerala lies to the west of Tamil Nadu.
2. Geographical Features
  • Coastal Plains: Tamil Nadu features a broad coastal plain along its eastern side, characterized by sandy beaches and fertile delta regions.
  • Hills and Mountains: The state has several mountain ranges, including the Western Ghats (which run along the western border) and the Nilgiri Hills. The Anaimalai Hills and Cardamom Hills are also part of this range.
  • Rivers: Major rivers include the Kaveri, Vaigai, Godavari, and Periyar, which play a crucial role in agriculture and irrigation.
3. Climate
  • Tropical Climate: Tamil Nadu has a predominantly tropical climate, with hot and humid conditions.
  • Monsoon Seasons: The southwest monsoon (June to September) and the northeast monsoon (October to December) bring significant rainfall, particularly to the eastern coastal areas.
4. Regions
  • Delta Region: The Kaveri Delta is one of the most fertile areas in the state, known for its agriculture and rice cultivation.
  • Urban Centers: Major cities include Chennai (the capital), Coimbatore, Madurai, Tiruchirappalli, and Salem, each contributing to the state's economic and cultural landscape.
5. Flora and Fauna
  • Flora and Fauna: Tamil Nadu is home to diverse ecosystems, including coastal areas, forests, and hill stations, which support various flora and fauna. Protected areas such as the Mudumalai National Park and Anamalai Tiger Reserve preserve wildlife and natural habitats.
Conclusion

Tamil Nadu's geographical location, with its coastal plains, mountain ranges, and fertile river valleys, significantly influences its climate, agriculture, and cultural practices. The state's diverse landscapes offer a rich tapestry of natural beauty and resources.
