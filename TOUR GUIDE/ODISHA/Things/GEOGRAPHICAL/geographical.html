GEOGRAPHICAL LOCATION

Geographical Location of Odisha

Odisha is located on the eastern coast of India, bordered by the Bay of Bengal to the east, and it is one of the 28 states of India. It is positioned in such a way that it acts as a bridge between the northern and southern parts of India, making it a strategically important state in the region.

Here’s a breakdown of its geographical location:

1. Borders
  • North: Odisha shares its northern boundary with West Bengal and Jharkhand.
  • South: It is bordered by Andhra Pradesh to the south.
  • West: Odisha shares its western border with Chhattisgarh.
  • East: The state has a coastline along the Bay of Bengal, stretching about 485 km.
2. Coordinates
  • Latitude: 17.78° N to 20.94° N
  • Longitude: 81.37° E to 87.29° E
3. Divisions

Odisha is divided into 30 districts, which are further grouped into 4 revenue divisions:

  • Bhubaneswar
  • Cuttack
  • Sambalpur
  • Berhampur

Each division serves as an administrative region to help govern the state's diverse geography and population.

4. Terrain and Topography

Odisha has a diverse landscape that includes:

  • Coastal Plains: The eastern boundary of the state consists of the coastal plains along the Bay of Bengal, with beaches and deltaic regions formed by major rivers like the Mahanadi.
  • Mountain Ranges: The Eastern Ghats, a series of mountain ranges, run along the western part of the state. These hills are home to a variety of flora and fauna and play a crucial role in the state's climate.
  • River Basins: Odisha is blessed with several rivers, including the Mahanadi, Brahmani, Baitarani, Rusikulya, and Sabarmati rivers, which irrigate the land and support agriculture.
  • Plateau Regions: The central and western parts of the state consist of plateau regions that are rich in minerals and resources.
5. Climate

Odisha has a tropical climate with high humidity due to its proximity to the Bay of Bengal.

The state experiences three primary seasons:

  • Summer (March to June): Hot and humid with temperatures often exceeding 40°C (104°F) in some parts.
  • Monsoon (June to September): The state receives heavy rainfall, particularly along the coast.
  • Winter (November to February): The temperature becomes mild and pleasant, ranging from 15°C to 30°C (59°F to 86°F), making it the most comfortable season.
6. Important Geographic Features
  • Chilika Lake: The largest coastal lagoon in India and one of the largest in the world, located along the coast of Odisha. It is a UNESCO Ramsar site and is rich in biodiversity.
  • Simlipal National Park: A protected reserve located in the northern part of Odisha, known for its forested hills and wildlife, including tigers and elephants.
  • Konark: Known for the famous Konark Sun Temple, it is located in the coastal region and offers stunning views of the Bay of Bengal.
Conclusion

Odisha's geographical location along the Bay of Bengal, with its rich rivers, coastal plains, plateaus, and mountainous regions, offers diverse ecological and cultural landscapes. The state's strategic position makes it an important center for trade, tourism, and cultural heritage in eastern India.
