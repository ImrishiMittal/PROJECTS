FOOD

Bihar's cuisine is a delightful blend of flavors, ingredients, and cooking techniques that reflect the state's rich cultural heritage and agricultural abundance. The food of Bihar is primarily vegetarian, though non-vegetarian dishes are also consumed in certain communities. Here’s a closer look at some of the most popular traditional foods from Bihar:

Litti Chokha
  • Litti: These are round wheat balls stuffed with roasted gram flour (sattu) mixed with spices and herbs. They are traditionally cooked in a tandoor (clay oven), giving them a distinct smoky flavor.
  • Chokha: This is a dish made from mashed vegetables like eggplant (baingan), potatoes (aloo), and tomatoes, seasoned with mustard oil, garlic, and spices. Litti Chokha is a staple dish and is often enjoyed with pickles and green chutney.
Sattu
  • Sattu: Roasted gram flour is a versatile ingredient used in various dishes. It can be consumed as a drink (sattu sharbat) mixed with water, lemon, and sugar or used to make stuffed parathas (flatbreads) and other snacks.
  • Sattu Paratha: These are unleavened flatbreads stuffed with spiced sattu and served with yogurt or pickles.
Bihari Khichdi
  • Khichdi: This is a one-pot dish made of rice and lentils, often cooked with spices and vegetables. It is commonly served during festivals and is considered a comfort food, especially on Makar Sankranti.
Dal Pitha
  • Dal Pitha: These are rice dumplings filled with spiced lentil paste. They can be steamed or boiled and are often served with chutney or curry. They are especially popular during the winter months.
Kheer
  • Kheer: A traditional sweet dish made from rice, milk, and sugar, often flavored with cardamom and garnished with nuts and raisins. Kheer is commonly prepared during festivals and special occasions.
Thekua
  • Thekua: A sweet snack made from wheat flour, jaggery, and grated coconut, shaped into small discs and deep-fried. Thekua is especially popular during festivals like Chhath Puja.
Puri and Sabzi
  • Puri: Deep-fried bread made from wheat flour, often served with various vegetable curries (sabzi). This combination is a common choice for festive meals and special occasions.
Chura (Flattened Rice)
  • Chura: Flattened rice is often eaten with curd, jaggery, or mixed with spices and vegetables. It’s a popular snack during festivals and is enjoyed for breakfast or as a light meal.
Fish Curry
  • Fish Curry: While vegetarian food dominates Bihari cuisine, certain communities enjoy non-vegetarian dishes, including fish curry made with freshwater fish like rohu, seasoned with spices and mustard oil.
Bihari Sweets
  • Makhana Kheer: A delicious dessert made from fox nuts (makhana) cooked in milk and sweetened with sugar.
  • Kesar Peda: A sweet made from khoya (reduced milk), flavored with saffron (kesar) and cardamom.
Conclusion

Bihar's food is a reflection of its rich agricultural heritage and cultural diversity. The use of locally available ingredients, traditional cooking methods, and vibrant flavors make Bihari cuisine unique and flavorful. Whether it’s the hearty Litti Chokha, the sweetness of Thekua, or the comforting warmth of Khichdi, the food of Bihar is a celebration of its culture and traditions, often enjoyed in the company of family and friends during festive occasions and gatherings.
